Coconut Curry Stir Fry

This recipe is one of my favorites. Once you've built up your knife skills, it's quick and easy to prepare and then pull together. And if you're newer to cooking, it's a great place to practice. This is a simple, customizable recipe that is still packed full of flavor.

In the recipe, I'm going to use chicken thighs for my protein. They're a personal favorite, juicer than breasts, and a lot harder to overcook and dry out. But swapping to a protein like tofu or steak is simple. For tougher meats just remember to slice them thinly. In addition, the recipe is going to use lo mein noodles for a starch. You could easily replace with rice, or any other type of noodle, or completely omit all together.

Once you understand the basic process of stir frying, it's easy to tweak to your tastes. While I'm going to give amounts for this recipe, I highly recommend you taste as you go and add more or less as you feel. You're making this for you.

Ingredients

For the Sauce

  • 1 can Plain Unsweetened Coconut Milk
  • 1 1/2 tbsp Curry Paste
  • 1 tbsp Curry Powder
  • 1 tbsp Honey
  • Salt to taste (a pinch or two)

For the Stir Fry

  • 1 tbsp canola oil, or any neutral cooking oil
  • 1LB chicken thigh, boneless and skinless, thinly sliced
  • 1 tsp minced garlic
  • 1 tsp minced ginger
  • 1 thinly sliced onion
  • 2 thinly sliced bell peppers
  • 1 carrot sliced in to thin strips
  • Lo mein noodles, store bought, prepared following their instructions
  • Coconut curry sauce, as made above
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Thai basil for garnish, if hard to find, you can skip

Steps

For the Sauce

  1. Combine all the ingredients in a pot
  2. Bring to a low boil, and let simmer.
  3. Taste to adjust spice level
  4. Leave simmering while preparing everything else.
  5. The sauce can be made, cooled, and stored ahead of time.

For the Stir Fry

  1. Prepare your lo mein noodles as instructed and set aside
  2. Warm up a large skillet or wok.
  3. Add the oil, and after letting warm up for a few seconds, add the chicken, and cook for three to five minutes, until just cooked
  4. Remove the chicken from the pan, leaving a tiny bit of the liquid
  5. Add the garlic and ginger
  6. Once you start smelling the ginger and garlic, add your onions, peppers, and carrot
  7. Cook on high heat, stirring or shaking often. Once the vegetable start to soften, maybe two or three minutes, add the chicken back to the pan
  8. Add the the sauce to the meat and vegetables, and mix. Taste for any additional salt and pepper
  9. You may add your cooked noodles to the mix now and combine, or keep aside and serve seperately
  10. Add your thai basil for garnish, and enjoy
